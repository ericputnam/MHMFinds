## Patreon post

**Title:** We unlocked the CAS camera's vertical angle. EA had it the whole time.

**Body:**

A week after we shipped the trait pack, our writer Felister came back from
making a lookbook with a complaint we'd all had and never said out loud: the
CAS camera only rotates sideways. No overhead shot. No low angle. Every
outfit photographed at the same polite eye level, forever.

So we went digging in the game files, and here's the thing — the vertical
angle was never locked by the engine. Every CAS camera preset has a pitch
value sitting in a config table. EA just keeps them all between -7 and +10
degrees. Change the number, tilt the camera.

Lookbook Camera is three variants in one download. You install exactly one
at a time:

- Top-Down — +55 degrees. Overhead editorial, flat-lay energy.
- Editorial — +22 degrees. The catalog angle. Your Sim, but photographed.
- Runway — -18 degrees. Shot from below. Every Sim looks ten feet tall.

Only the full-body views change. Face and detail editing are completely
stock, and horizontal rotation still works everywhere, so you're orbiting
your Sim 360 at whatever height you picked.

Two honest notes. First, true free vertical orbit — click and drag up and
down — isn't possible without patching the game's executable, which we
won't do. Fixed angles you swap per shoot is the real ceiling, and in
practice it covers the lookbook use case. Second, this overrides the game's
camera settings, so it conflicts with other CAS camera mods and you should
grab a fresh copy after major patches.

Download: https://musthavemods.com/go/sims-4-lookbook-camera-mod/

Unzip, drop ONE .package into your Mods folder, restart, open CAS. If the
angle isn't for you, swap the file and restart — it's about 1KB.

If you shoot a lookbook with this, we genuinely want to see it.
