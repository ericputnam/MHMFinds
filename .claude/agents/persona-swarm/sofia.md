# Sofia - User Persona

## Overview

Sofia is a representative user persona for MHMFinds, embodying the TikTok-driven Gen Z Sims 4 modder who prioritizes aesthetic trends and budget-conscious choices.

## Demographics

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Name | Sofia |
| Age | 22 |
| Location | Sao Paulo, Brazil |
| Annual Income | $25,000 USD |
| Occupation | University student / Part-time retail |
| Primary Language | Portuguese (fluent English) |

## Platform Behavior

### Discovery Channels
- **Primary**: TikTok (follows Sims creators, discovers mods through Sims 4 CC haul videos)
- **Secondary**: Pinterest (saves aesthetic boards), Instagram Reels
- **Tertiary**: YouTube (tutorial content, mod showcases)

### Device Usage
- Mobile-first (80% of browsing on smartphone)
- Uses laptop for actual gameplay and mod installation
- Prefers dark mode interfaces

### Browsing Patterns
- Short attention span (scrolls quickly, skips long descriptions)
- Highly visual (thumbnail quality determines clicks)
- Engages with trending content and aesthetic tags
- Saves items to collections for later download during gameplay sessions

## Aesthetic Preferences

### Primary Style: Y2K
Sofia gravitates toward early 2000s aesthetics:
- Low-rise jeans and butterfly tops
- Bedazzled accessories and chunky jewelry
- Platform shoes and mini skirts
- Baby tees and halter tops
- Bright colors, metallics, and holographic materials
- Butterfly clips and hair accessories

### Secondary Interests
- Streetwear and urban fashion
- Baddie aesthetic
- Soft glam makeup
- Trendy nail sets
- Modern minimalist furniture (for Sim apartments)

### Content Types of Interest
- tops - Crop tops, baby tees, halter tops
- bottoms - Low-rise jeans, mini skirts, cargo pants
- full-body - Complete Y2K outfits
- jewelry - Chunky chains, butterfly accessories
- shoes - Platform sneakers, chunky sandals
- makeup - Glossy lips, soft glam
- hair - Trendy styles with accessories

## Budget Behavior

### Spending Habits
- **Free content priority**: Always checks free options first
- **Occasional purchases**: Will spend 5-15 dollars on highly-desired paid content
- **Bundle preference**: Looks for value packs over individual items
- **Patreon hesitancy**: Reluctant to commit to monthly subscriptions

### Price Sensitivity
| Price Point | Likelihood to Purchase |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Free | High (primary choice) |
| 1-3 dollars | Moderate (impulse buy) |
| 4-7 dollars | Low (requires strong desire) |
| 8+ dollars | Very Low (special occasions) |
| Subscription | Very Low (prefers one-time) |

### Value Signals
- Looks for pack or set in titles (more items = better value)
- Attracted to free tags prominently displayed
- Checks download counts as social proof
- Responds to limited-time free releases

## Search Behavior

### Typical Search Queries
- y2k cc
- y2k clothes sims 4
- 2000s fashion cc
- butterfly clips cc
- low rise jeans sims 4
- baddie cc pack
- aesthetic cc free
- trendy sims 4 cc

### Filter Usage
- **Always filters by**: Price (Free first), Content Type
- **Often filters by**: Themes (Y2K, streetwear), Visual Style
- **Rarely filters by**: Pack requirements, Age groups

### Browsing Journey
1. Arrives via TikTok link or search
2. Scrolls rapidly through thumbnails
3. Opens items with appealing visuals in new tabs
4. Checks price immediately (closes if too expensive)
5. Skims images (rarely reads full description)
6. Downloads if free, or adds to wishlist if paid
7. Returns during weekend gaming sessions to batch download

## Engagement Patterns

### Time on Site
- Average session: 5-10 minutes
- Peak activity: Evenings (8pm-11pm local time)
- Weekend spikes: Saturday afternoons

### Actions per Session
- Views: 15-25 mod pages
- Downloads: 3-5 free items
- Saves/Favorites: 5-10 items
- Purchases: 0-1 (rare)

### Return Behavior
- Returns 2-3 times per week
- Checks New and Trending sections
- Revisits saved items when budget allows

## Pain Points

### Frustrations
- Broken download links
- Clickbait thumbnails (mod does not match preview)
- Hidden prices or unexpected paywalls
- Slow mobile performance
- Too many clicks to download
- Required email signups

### Dealbreakers
- Mandatory account creation for browsing
- Pop-up ads
- Unclear pack requirements
- No preview images

## Opportunities

### How to Win Sofia
1. **Fast mobile experience**: Instant loading thumbnails
2. **Clear pricing**: Free badge prominently visible
3. **Aesthetic filtering**: Y2K/2000s theme filters
4. **Social proof**: Download counts, trending indicators
5. **Visual-first**: Large, high-quality preview images
6. **Easy discovery**: TikTok-style scrolling, Similar items
7. **Collection support**: Easy save-for-later functionality

### Conversion Potential
- High engagement, low immediate conversion
- Potential for affiliate revenue through free content
- May convert to paid user as income grows
- Strong word-of-mouth potential (shares finds on TikTok)
