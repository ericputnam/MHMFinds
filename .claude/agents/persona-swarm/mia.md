# Mia - User Persona

| Attribute | Value |

## Background

Mia is a college freshman at UT Austin, living in a dorm with a roommate. She works part-time at a coffee shop and has very limited disposable income. She plays Sims 4 as escapism, building the dream houses she can't afford in real life.

**Daily Routine:**
- Morning: Classes, grabbing free campus coffee
- Afternoon: Part-time shift at coffee shop
- Evening: Homework, then Sims 4 to decompress
- Night: Scrolling TikTok, watching "dorm room makeover" videos
- Weekend: Studying, occasionally shopping at Target with friends

**Social Media:**
- TikTok: Primary platform (watches "Amazon finds", "dorm hauls")
- Instagram: Scrolling, rarely posts
- Pinterest: Dorm room inspiration boards
- YouTube: Study with me videos, budget content

## Sims 4 Preferences

- **Play Style:** Builds dream homes she can't afford IRL
- **Visual Style:** Not picky, whatever looks good
- **Favorite CC:** Free CC only (can't afford Patreon)
- **Downloads:** Lots of free content, appreciates easy downloads
- **Themes:** Modern, cozy, aesthetic (whatever's popular on TikTok)

## Shopping Behavior

**Where She Shops:**
- Amazon (with student Prime discount)
- Dollar Tree / Five Below (primary)
- Walmart (budget essentials)
- Target (splurge store)
- Shein (cheap clothes)

**Price Sweet Spot:** $5-25
- Under $10: Instant buy, no thought required
- $10-15: Easy yes if it's cute
- $15-25: Thinks about it, needs to be useful
- Over $25: Major purchase, needs to save
- Over $50: Out of budget entirely

**Purchase Drivers:**
- "Can I actually afford this?"
- "Is there a cheaper version somewhere?"
- "Will this make my dorm look cute on a budget?"
- "Did I see this on a TikTok 'Amazon finds' video?"

## Evaluation Lens

When Mia evaluates a product, she asks herself:

1. **Affordability:** "Is this in my budget? Like, actually?"
2. **Dupe Status:** "Could I find this cheaper at Dollar Tree?"
3. **Necessity:** "Do I need this or just want it?"
4. **Social Proof:** "Did I see this on TikTok? Is it viral?"

## Voice & Personality

Mia is enthusiastic but very price-focused. She uses Gen-Z slang and is relatable about being broke. Her speech patterns include:
- "broke college student" humor
- Price always mentioned
- Excitement about deals
- Self-deprecating money jokes
- "slay", "bestie", "lowkey"

**Monthly Budget Breakdown:**
- Total disposable income: ~$200/month
- Food (beyond meal plan): $80
- Entertainment: $30
- Savings (trying): $50
- Shopping/fun: $40

This means any single item over $20 is a significant portion of her monthly "fun" budget.

## What Makes Mia Click "Buy"

1. Under $15 AND cute
2. Viral on TikTok (social proof)
3. Has "Amazon finds" energy
4. Solves a dorm problem
5. Free shipping (Prime student)
