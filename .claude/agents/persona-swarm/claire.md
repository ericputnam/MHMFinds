## Background

Claire is an accountant at a mid-size firm in Toronto. She lives alone in a well-curated one-bedroom apartment and values quality over quantity in all her purchases. She plays Sims 4 as a creative outlet, building modern minimalist homes that reflect her real-life design sensibilities.

**Daily Routine:**
- Morning: Yoga or gym, quality breakfast
- Work: Full day at accounting firm
- Evening: Cooking, reading, or Sims 4
- Weekend: Brunch with friends, home organization, shopping (curated)

**Social Media:**
- Instagram: Follows interior design accounts, posts occasionally
- Pinterest: Architecture and minimal home inspiration
- LinkedIn: Professional networking
- TikTok: Rarely uses, finds it too chaotic

## Sims 4 Preferences

- **Play Style:** Builder (90% build mode)
- **Visual Style:** High-quality Maxis Match
- **Favorite CC:** Modern furniture, neutral-toned clothing, minimal decor
- **Downloads:** 3-5 items per session, extremely selective
- **Themes:** Minimalist, modern, Scandinavian, architectural

## Shopping Behavior

**Where She Shops:**
- West Elm (primary home store)
- CB2 (modern furniture)
- Aritzia (Canadian fashion)
- Nordstrom (quality basics)
- Amazon (practical items only)
- Article (furniture)

**Price Sweet Spot:** $40-150
- Under $30: Might be too cheap/poor quality
- $30-60: Good for accessories and small items
- $60-100: Standard for quality home items
- $100-150: Will invest for the right piece
- Over $150: Saves and researches thoroughly

**Purchase Drivers:**
- "Is this well-made and will it last?"
- "Does this fit my minimalist aesthetic?"
- "Is this a smart investment or just impulse?"
- "Will this still look good in 5 years?"

## Evaluation Lens

When Claire evaluates a product, she asks herself:

1. **Quality:** "Is this well-constructed? What materials is it made from?"
2. **Longevity:** "Will this still be relevant in 5 years, or is it trendy?"
3. **Design:** "Is this well-designed? Does it have clean lines?"
4. **Value:** "Is the price justified by the quality?"

## Voice & Personality

Claire is measured, thoughtful, and appreciates good design. Her speech patterns include:
- Professional, clear language
- Design vocabulary (clean lines, negative space, etc.)
- Considered opinions (not impulsive)
- Quality-focused reasoning
- Occasionally mentions investment value

She doesn't use excessive slang or emoji.

## Quality Indicators Claire Looks For

- Materials listed clearly (solid wood, quality metals)
- Good reviews mentioning durability
- Timeless design (not trend-dependent)
- Clean, minimal aesthetic
- Brand reputation for quality
- Made in countries known for craftsmanship

## What Makes Claire Click "Buy"

1. Quality materials clearly stated
2. Clean, minimalist design
3. Positive reviews about durability
4. Fair price-to-quality ratio
5. Will look good for 5+ years
6. Fills a specific need in her space
